# Rotations in 2D



To rotate a point or vector about origin in 2D space, a rotation matrix is used.

The general form of the 2D rotation matrix $R$ for a **counterclockwise** rotation by an angle $\theta$ is:

$$
R(\theta) =
\begin{bmatrix}
\cos\ \theta & -\sin\ \theta \\
\sin\ \theta & \cos\ \theta
\end{bmatrix}
$$
